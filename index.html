Mihir Shah's home page

Hi, I'm Mihir. I am a software engineer at NVIDIA, working on compilers for deep learning, targeting GPUs. I graduated from UT Austin with a B.S. in computer science, in the Turing Scholars program.

Overall, I am interested in building systems that leverage novel hardware architectures (CPU, GPU, SmartNIC, programmable switch, etc.) to support modern data-intensive applications (deep learning, databases, etc). I like all areas of systems spanning this area- computer architecture, heterogeneity, compilers, operating systems, databases, low-level networking, etc.

During my B.S., I was in the UT networked systems lab, advised by Prof. Aditya Akella. My undergrad thesis was improving P4DB. P4DB uses the Intel Tofino programmable switch to accelerate a distributed OLTP database. My project was modifying P4DB's database protocols to enable rearranging database interactions with the switch, to improve switch memory utilization by an order of magnitude.
